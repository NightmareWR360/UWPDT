Phi Delt Nationwide

Phi Delta Theta was founded on three pillars that define the environment and attitude of the Fraternity, these three pillars are: The cultivation of friendship among its members, the acquirement individually of a high degree of mental culture, and the attainment personally of a high standard of morality. Or, the shortened version: friendship, sound learning, and moral rectitude. Founded in 1848 at Miami University campus in Oxford, Ohio Phi Delta Theta has grown immensely with nearly 190 active chapters and colonies in 42 states and five Canadian provinces. The Fraternity has initiated more than 250,000 men and currently has more than 160,000 living alumni. Check out this list of famous Phis, I think you’ll know a few

Phi Delt at UW

The Wisconsin Alpha chapter of Phi Delta Theta is back now with more than 50 committed brothers after just reinstalling during the 2015-2016 school year. With brothers from a wide array of backgrounds and academic goals, Phi Delta Theta at the University of Wisconsin-Madison seeks to renew the tradition set by our predecessors and to provide a strong and positive influence within the Greek community.

Why rush Phi Delt?

With extremely active local alumni, Phi Delt not only offers opportunities for professional networking, but also an environment with active intramurals and social events. Although we do not currently have a Fraternity house, our housing corporation has been actively pursuing multiple properties in preparation for the 2017-2018 school year.
